This paragraph will contain strong vs bold. They both have ending tags. Ok so strong and bold looks the exact same; however, the purpose behind it is different. The semantics of it all! Bold is when you want attention to a word. Like I need to buy eggs, oatmilk, and bananas. I put bold markers on each word. Now strong is when you want you want to convey something important like stand clear of the closing doors please . You know like in the subways! I believe screen readers interpret these differences differently so yeah still important! One invokes a feeling, other is purely show.

Just like the last tags, we have two tags that display the same, but the semantics/meaning is different. We have Emphasis vs Italics. One is a feeling, the other is simply for looks. Now emphasis is when you want to really empahize what you are saying. We really need to get lock in. That's emphaszing that we need to focus. For Italics, we use it seperate a word from text, just to show it's different. Like quoting or name of book, those things. Obama once said "Yes we can." It's a quote so I wanted to offset it from the rest of the text.

Now there is something I didn't cover. I've been putting some of these Paragraphs in divs. Why? Well sometimes you want to group texts to together to give them a sense of style. I have 2 divs. One div that has the text formats (bold, italics, strong, emph). The other div is around the kinds of lists. Each of my divs has two paragraphs. I'm not styling them now, but if I wanted to, I could style them! Div 1, the text formats would be styled the same. Div 2 would be styled next with their own kind of style. So I have all these paragrpahs, but since they are grouped in their own divs, they will take the style of their divs. Oh, and divs can be nested. Nested means when you put a tag in another tag.
